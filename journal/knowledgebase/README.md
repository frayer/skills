# Knowledge Base

This directory contains reference files that provide organizational context for your work journal. Customize them to reflect your actual company and team — the more specific they are, the more useful they become as grounding material.

## Files

### `objectives.md`

The objectives driving your work — corporate, team, or personal. Describes what you're trying to accomplish and how success is measured. Used to align weekly notes, reviews, and advice to your actual priorities rather than generic language.

### `principles.md`

The principles that guide your work — technical standards, engineering practices, ways of working, or any other values that shape how you and your team operate. Used to ground framing and advice in your real context.

## Customizing

Replace the sample content in each file with your actual organizational content:

- **Objectives** — use language you actually use day-to-day; keep it to 2–4 objectives with concrete key results
- **Principles** — reflect what actually shapes your decisions; 4–6 principles is the right range

Keep the files concise. They are read in full, so focus on what matters most.

## Maintenance

Review and refresh these files when organizational priorities or technical direction shifts — roughly quarterly, or after major strategy or architecture changes.
